üéØ Read and React Philosophy

Core Philosophy

Read and React = Relentlessly attack defense weak areas without pattern and always take what it's given by defense

Read and React = Emphasize intelligence (shot selection, pass and drives timing, patience with ball - choose best action)

Embracing freestyle (motion) -- constant movement mandatory for all 5 positions

Coaching Design Principles

  • Design drills with every action ending with scoring option
  • Example: Pass and Cut drill - receiver catches and shoots, cutter gets pass from 3rd player, then reverse or extend to two actions before first or second player attacks
  • Tape practice: Meet with team before practice, tell them what they're about to do
  • Set goals: Before games/practices, establish team goals, individual goals, areas of emphasis (grab 10 rebounds, stop penetration, don't allow opponent to score 80 points, etc.)

üìö Glossary & Terminology

Court Positions & Areas

  • Corner: Baseline corner areas
  • Wing (W): Side court positions
  • Point/Top of Key (T): Top of 3-point arc
  • Short Corner: Area between baseline and free throw line extended
  • High Post: Free throw line area
  • Mid Post: Between high and low post
  • Low Post: Block area
  • Elbow (E): Free throw line corners

Actions & Movements

  • Cut (C): Movement toward basket
  • Front Cut (FC): Cut in front of defender
  • Back Cut (BC)/Rear Cut: Cut behind defender
  • X-Cut: Crossing cut pattern
  • LA Cut: Low/High basket cut options
  • Down Screen (DS): Screen toward baseline
  • Up Screen/Back Screen (BS): Screen toward half court
  • Dribble At (At): Dribbling directly at teammate
  • Read Line: 3-point line as decision marker
  • Skip Pass: Pass across court (two or more passes away)
  • Feed the Post: Pass into post position

Defensive Terms

  • Defender (D): Defensive player
  • Over: Defender over the screen
  • Under: Defender under the screen
  • Switch: Defenders exchange assignments
  • Through: Defender fights through screen
  • ICE: Force ball handler to sideline

Formation Calls

  • Red: 5 out formation
  • Green: 4/3 out, 1/2 in formation

‚≠ê Points of Emphasis

Fundamental Requirements

  • Spacing and spots - Proper floor positioning
  • Timing - Change of speed for most R&R actions
  • Constant movement - Never stand still
  • Communication - Always use names then call or show action
  • Always be ready to attack - With ball or without it
  • Strong and sharp passing - Pass away from defender
  • Must move to the pass - Don't wait for ball to come to you
  • Read defenders - Take all that defense gives you
  • Hunt for better position, not the ball

Essential Skills & Techniques

  • Move towards pass - Not standing still waiting for ball
  • Triple threat position - Every time you catch the ball
  • Always first thing is to pump fake
  • Use fakes: Pass fake, jab step, pivots
  • Sharp passing - If someone can't catch, throw even harder next time
  • Be tough: If defender prevents you going left, "swim over" and go right - get physical

Drill Progression Example

Pass and Cut progression:

  • 1 cuts left, 2 cuts right
  • Chest pass, bounce pass, layup pass
  • Finish reverse, finish jump shot, finish tear drop
  • Hook pass, behind the back pass, etc.
  • Next level: Add defender
  • Next level: Add second ball for extra shot practice

Evaluation Method

Scrimmage 5 vs 0: Ask players to do any type of action continuously. As coach, observe what they prefer to do more or less, then ask directly why they prefer one thing over another. This helps identify what to emphasize or address in next practice.

1. Pass and Cut

Basic Rules:

  • If you pass, you must cut
  • Pass one pass away ‚Üí Must Cut (Front Cut or Rear Cut)
  • Read Line/Defender: If defender crosses 3-point line, you must cut to basket
  • If defender looks away, you must cut
  • If defender not in defensive stance, you must cut

Formation Options:

  • 5 out: All perimeter players
  • 4 out 1 in or 3 out 2 in: Post players included

Cutting Fundamentals:

  • Ask for ball while cutting - demand ball on the cut
  • Show your hands - demonstrate you're open
  • Use names to call for ball
  • On cut - must touch spot underneath the basket

Filling Empty Spots:

  • Move to the ball - don't stand still and wait
  • Be ready to catch and shoot: Stride stop, jump stop
  • Fill spot moving away from 3-point line - curve away
  • Every time you get ball: Triple threat position
  • Every time you get ball: Fake a shot first

Finishing Options:

  • Pump fake, drop step, power layup
  • Euro step, jump stop, power layup
  • Catch one dribble, quick spin, layup
  • Catch and shoot
  • Catch ‚Üí sidestep ‚Üí shot

2. Read Line

3-Point Line as Decision Maker:

  • Defender crosses 3-point line ‚Üí Must cut
  • Defender overplays ‚Üí Must cut
  • Defender turns head away ‚Üí Must cut to basket
  • Defender in denying position ‚Üí Must cut to basket (rear cut)

Alternative Actions:

  • X-Cut: Crossing cut pattern (read vs overplay/deny reactions)
  • Pass and Screen: If can't get ball
  • Dribble Hand Off

3. Post Pass and Cut

Post Positions:

  • Short corner
  • Low post
  • Mid post
  • High post

Feeding Post Rules:

  • If you feed the post, you must basket cut
  • Cutter options: Go wide or brush screen-like
  • Wing feeds post and no one in corner: Regardless of cut direction, closest player must fill vacated spot
  • If passer has teammates on right and left: Direction of cut pulls closest player to vacated spot

Post Player Development:

  • Different passing types: Wrap around, push pass, bounce pass, late pass
  • LA Cut options: Low post LA high, mid or high post any LA cuts
  • X-Cut opportunities

4. Dribble At

Basic Rule:

  • Dribble At teammate ‚Üí Must basket cut (Power dribble is exception)
  • Dribble At Ball Side High/Mid Post ‚Üí Must basket cut then short corner
  • Dribble At Weak Side High/Mid Post ‚Üí Must basket cut then short corner

üéØ Advanced Concepts

Circle Move Strategy

Ball Handler Drives:

  • Drive right or left ‚Üí Everyone on perimeter moves one spot right or left
  • Ball handler changes direction ‚Üí Circle movement must change direction
  • Safety valve denied ‚Üí Must basket cut or X-Cut
  • Penetrator who passed ball ‚Üí May choose any R&R action (screen, finish cut, post up, etc.)

Basic Post Slides

Post Player Rules:

  • Any player near paint or short corner ‚Üí Must obey post player rules if drive happens
  • Ball dribbles above post (strong/weak side) ‚Üí Must slide to short corner
  • Ball dribbles below post (strong/weak side) ‚Üí Step above FT line, elbow

Baseline Drive

Condition: Only if no teammate between you and baseline

When Drive Occurs, Everyone Must Fill:

  • Fill opposite corner (circular movement first, then back to corner)
  • 45-degree angle (if no post player on line)
  • 90-degree angle (if no post player on line)
  • Safety valve

Pin & Skip

Skills: Read weak side defenders, effective against zone, last-second shots

Rules and Situations:

  • MUST communicate - Name of player then "PIN!"
  • Pin defender with longest recovery
  • 4 out: Low post weak side automatic screen
  • Ball on top, 5 out: Wing can set pin for corner and vice versa
  • 4 out ball side: Low/mid post sets pin screen for wing

Next Best Action:

  • After basket, make pin screen (weak side)

Circle Reverse

When Penetrator Picks Up Dribble:

  • Natural pitch player can reverse circle
  • Get in position as safety valve
  • Behave as post player executing advanced slides

Safety Valve Player Reactions:

  • Defender in deny position or crossed 3-point line ‚Üí LA cut low or high
  • Execute X-Cut to open next player
  • If perimeter player can't get ball ‚Üí MUST CUT to vacate spot

Power Dribble

Signal for hand-off pick and roll from weak side player:

  • Create mismatch
  • Pressure relief
  • Post advance

Relief Player Reads:

  • Defender pressing ball handler, denying one direction ‚Üí Drop step or spin
  • Defender goes under ‚Üí Shape up for shot or dribble drive
  • Defender in deny ‚Üí Curl shoulder to shoulder
  • Defender over read line ‚Üí Must basket cut (read line rule)

Screening Actions

Post Screens:

  • Post player must obey Post Slides or Advanced Post Slides rules
  • Back screen, Flare screen, Ball screen - all freedoms of action for post players

Back Screen Options:

  • After finishing basket cut, instead of filling open spot: Back screen or Ball screen
  • For post player to get out of paint: May use any type of screen

Multiple Screen Rule:

  • Never set a screen and stand
  • Options after screen: Post up, Flash open area, Fill open spot

  • 3 players out: Left/Right Wings and Top
  • 5 players out: Left/Right Wings, Top, both Corners

Read and React Transition Options

  • Fill out to open spot (weak side or strong side)
  • Post up ‚Üí Feed post LA-Cut, X-Cut
  • Set a Pin Screen
  • Use a Pin and Screen
  • After Cut set a Back Screen
  • Use X-Cut or Relocate to open spot

üèÜ Competitive Drilling

Read Line Competitive 4-on-4 or 5-on-5

Setup Rules:

  • Do not allow mixing stages until every stage is mastered separately
  • Offense: 5 out, one meter away from arc, everyone in athletic stance
  • We don't fake cut - we just cut!
  • Spacing: Draw defense away from paint
  • Don't bring extra defender to ball

Defense Requirements:

  • Man-to-man pressure on ball all the time
  • One pass away: Guard passing line or full body deny
  • Two passes away: Help position
  • Weak side: Guard paint against drives

Defensive Reactions:

  • A) Defender over-aggressive, stepping over 3-point line ‚Üí Offense must cut
  • B) Defender plays safe, gets ball ‚Üí Must execute requested move (back/front pivot, pump fake, rip through, jab step, etc.)

üî¥ Offense Scoring

  • 1 point: Every successful pass on perimeter
  • 2 points: Every successful pass to paint
  • 3 points: Every successful layup
  • 2 points: Every offensive rebound

üîµ Defense Scoring

  • 1 point: Deflection one pass away (no turnover)
  • 2 points: Deflection one pass away (turnover)
  • 1 point: Deflection skip pass (no turnover)
  • 2 points: Deflection skip pass (turnover)
  • 2 points: Every missed shot by offense
  • 2 points: Every defensive rebound
  • 3 points: Every missed layup

üéØ Offensive Stage Progression

  1. Stage 1: Pass ball one pass away, then must basket cut demanding ball. Players filling vacated spots must read defense and make moves based on defensive position (no fakes, no hesitations)
  2. Stage 2: After cut, can set back screens (must post up first, then execute action)
  3. Stage 3: Add Dribble At actions
  4. Stage 4: Add Drive actions (Corner cut, Natural Pitch, Bounce Off)
  5. Stage 5: Add Ball Screens
  6. Stage 6: Add side screens and X-Cuts
